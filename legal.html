// LEGAL & COMPLIANCE

Fractional general counsel for roughly zero dollars

Legal agents don't replace your lawyer — they replace the billable hours you'd spend getting your lawyer up to speed on a non-standard contract. The stack below cuts contract turnaround from weeks to hours and keeps your compliance posture audit-ready year-round.

contracts DPAs SOC2 privacy IP
01

Build a policy & template library first

This is the corpus every legal agent reads against.

  • Your own: MSA, DPA, SOW, NDA, offer letter, contractor agreement, privacy policy, AUP, terms of service, SLA.
  • Your positions: what you'll concede, what you won't, with reasons. This is the "playbook" document lawyers charge $10k to write — worth every dollar, once.
  • Regulatory: GDPR obligations, CCPA, state privacy laws you're subject to, any sector rules (HIPAA, PCI).
Store in git. Your legal agent becomes smarter every time your actual lawyer negotiates a term.
02

Contract-review agent

Consumes inbound contracts (MSAs, DPAs, customer redlines) and produces a structured review.

  • Compares clauses to your playbook: acceptable / negotiate / reject.
  • Highlights missing clauses your template would include.
  • Flags risky language: uncapped liability, broad IP assignment, unilateral termination, indemnification asymmetry.
  • Drafts redlines in the Word/Google Doc and a 5-bullet "what I'd push back on" memo.
Nothing goes to a customer without a human lawyer reviewing. The agent is a first pass; it gets you to the bar of "junior associate's first draft" in 10 minutes.
03

DPA & privacy agent

Enterprise deals live or die on the Data Processing Addendum.

  • Maintains a machine-readable list of your sub-processors with purpose and region.
  • Auto-fills customer DPA templates from your canonical data map.
  • Monitors sub-processor changes (did Stripe add a new region?) and notifies customers with active DPAs.
04

Security-questionnaire agent (the deal-accelerator)

The infamous SIG-Lite with 300 questions. This agent makes it a 2-hour task, not a 2-week one.

  • Reads against your policy library + Drata/Vanta evidence.
  • Drafts answers with citations to the source policy.
  • Flags questions where your current posture doesn't meet the ask — those go to a human, with a proposed remediation.
05

Clickwrap & consent agent

The sneakiest source of future liability: inconsistent terms.

  • Checks that every place a user can sign up, invite, or purchase presents the current ToS.
  • Logs consents with version hash of ToS; provides audit-ready exports on request.
  • When ToS changes, coordinates the re-consent flow across product + marketing surfaces.
06

IP hygiene agent

Startups die from sloppy IP. Not often, but when it happens, it's fatal at diligence.

  • Every employee and contractor has a signed IP assignment on file — agent tracks and nags until signed.
  • Open-source license scan on every build; blocks GPL/AGPL pulls into proprietary code.
  • Third-party content (images, fonts, code snippets) logged with license + source.
07

Regulatory radar

A scheduled agent that watches for changes in the rules you're subject to.

  • EU AI Act, state privacy laws (CA, CO, CT, VA, TX, UT…), sector rules.
  • When a relevant change hits, it produces a one-pager: what changed, who it affects, deadline, suggested action.
  • Routes to the founder + your outside counsel.
08

Incident-response & breach-notification playbook

Pre-written, rehearsed, agent-assisted. You don't want to be authoring a breach notice at 2am.

  • Decision tree: is this reportable? under which law? by when?
  • Notification templates per jurisdiction.
  • Agent assembles the facts from logs + ticket system; lawyer approves; comms sends.
Relationship model: outside counsel charges for judgment, not for drafting. Agents eat the drafting; your lawyer becomes 3x more valuable to you. Call them for the judgment calls and the signatures.
