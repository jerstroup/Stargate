Your AI co-founder before you can afford one

You are CEO, CFO, Chief of Staff, recruiter, and lead salesperson. This playbook turns three agents into the other six hats you're wearing — pitching, researching, negotiating, and reporting — so you can focus on the one thing only you can do: decide.

The agent roster

Four agents cover 90% of a founder's weekly work. Build them in this order.

01

Build a "Founder Context Pack" first

Every good founder agent needs three documents to stop hallucinating about your company. Put these in a single repo or Notion space — every agent reads from it.

  • one-pager.md — problem, customer, wedge, why-now, team, ask.
  • market.md — TAM/SAM/SOM with sources, 5 closest competitors, differentiation matrix.
  • metrics.md — current MRR/ARR, growth rate, burn, runway, CAC, top customer logos.
Update weekly. Treat it as a living artifact. Every agent prompt starts with <context>{{founder_context_pack}}</context>.
Example: see examples/founder.md — a filled-in one-pager.md for a fictional startup.
02

Pitch Deck & Narrative Agent

Turns your context pack into investor-ready decks, updates them for each fund's thesis, and rehearses you with devil's-advocate questions.

  • Tool: Claude Agent SDK + a Google Slides or Pitch.com MCP server.
  • Skill 1: generate_deck(fund_thesis, stage) — rewrites the narrative for sector investors vs generalists.
  • Skill 2: redteam_deck() — simulates the 20 hardest questions you'll get. Use before every partner meeting.
  • Skill 3: tighten_story() — forces your one-liner to under 140 chars and your problem slide to one sentence.
# Example: red-team the deck
claude --agent pitch-agent \
  --input deck.pdf \
  --prompt "You are a Series A partner at a16z focused on dev tools.
Ask me the 15 hardest questions about traction, moat, and GTM.
For each, score my deck's current answer 1-5 and suggest one fix."
03

Investor Research & CRM Agent

Maps the universe of investors for your stage/sector, scores them against your thesis, finds warm intros via LinkedIn/email graph, and keeps a running CRM.

  • Inputs: Crunchbase/PitchBook via MCP, LinkedIn, your Gmail + Calendar.
  • Output: ranked sheet of 80-120 funds with partner names, last relevant check, portfolio conflicts, and suggested intro path.
  • Loop: after every meeting, it pulls the calendar event, drafts a follow-up email, and logs outcome + next step.
Tip: let it draft outreach but gate the send. Investors spot generic AI copy in one line.
04

Market & Competitor Scout

A scheduled agent that runs every Monday morning and produces a 5-bullet "what changed in my market last week" brief.

  • Sources: Google News + RSS via WebFetch, competitor changelogs, HN, ProductHunt, YC batch pages, GitHub trending.
  • Uses a scheduled trigger (cron) + a summarizer sub-agent.
  • Delivers to Slack #intel channel — not email (emails die, channels compound).
# Weekly brief prompt (summarizer)
Produce 5 bullets, each <=2 sentences:
  1. Most important competitor move (with link)
  2. Shift in customer language we saw in reviews/social
  3. New fundraising in our space + thesis implied
  4. Regulatory or platform change that affects us
  5. One contrarian signal worth debating at Monday standup
05

Negotiation & Deal-Desk Agent

Reads inbound contracts (leases, term sheets, vendor MSAs), benchmarks terms against market, and drafts counters.

  • Lease negotiation: feed it the LOI + comparable rents scraped from CompStak/CoStar; it returns a markup with annotated comments.
  • Term sheets: compare to YC's Post-Money SAFE or NVCA templates; flag non-standard terms (option pool shuffles, participating pref, super pro-rata).
  • Vendor MSAs: auto-check for IP assignment, data handling, auto-renewal traps. Produce a one-page diff for your lawyer.
Guardrail: this agent never sends. It produces a markup + a 5-point "what I'd push back on" memo. You and your lawyer decide.
06

Personal Chief of Staff Agent

The glue agent. Reads your inbox + calendar, prepares you for every meeting, and drafts follow-ups within 10 minutes of each call ending.

  • Pre-brief: 30 min before a meeting, pings you in Slack with a one-pager: who they are, last interaction, three topics likely to come up, two asks.
  • Post-meeting: pulls transcripts (Fireflies/Granola MCP), extracts commitments, drafts the follow-up, and creates tasks in Linear/Notion.
  • Weekly review: Friday 4pm agent DM — "here's what slipped, here's what to cut, here's what I'd put on your calendar next week."
07

Rollout & governance

Three rules to stay sane:

  1. One agent a week. Ship, use it for 5 working days, fix, then add the next.
  2. Tag every agent-generated artifact. Footer: — drafted by pitch-agent v3, reviewed by [you]. Protects you legally and mentally.
  3. Audit monthly. Read the trace logs. Kill agents that aren't saving you 2+ hours/week.
