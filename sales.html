// SALES

Shorten the cycle from weeks to days

Founder-led sales is the best kind of sales — and the hardest to scale. Agents let the founder spend all their time on the 3 conversations that matter, while everything upstream (research, outreach, follow-up) runs in the background with human voice and human gates.

prospecting outbound call coaching deal desk
01

Define your ICP as a scoring rubric (not a paragraph)

An agent can't act on "enterprise SaaS, mid-market". Give it scores.

  • Firmographics: company size, vertical, tech stack, growth stage — each weighted.
  • Trigger events: new funding, new VP Eng, stack migration, specific job posting.
  • Disqualifiers: regulated industry you can't serve, cloud you don't support.
# icp.yaml
firmographics:
  employees: { range: [50, 500], weight: 2 }
  stack_has: [postgres, vercel], weight: 3
triggers:
  funding_announced_last_90_days: 4
  hiring_head_of_data: 5
disqualify:
  vertical: [defense, adult]
Example: see examples/sales.md — a filled-in icp.md (the prose companion to icp.yaml) for a fictional startup.
02

Prospect research agent

Takes an account + contact and returns a one-pager before every outbound message or call.

  • Public data: company news, funding, recent content by the contact, stack signals.
  • Internal data: prior touches, existing users at the company, NPS of those users.
  • Output: a "why this company now" angle in 1-2 sentences. If it can't produce one, skip the account.
03

Outbound drafting agent (never auto-send)

Drafts one-to-one outbound off the research brief. The word "personalized" means something here.

  • Opens with a specific, recent fact about the prospect (not "I've been following your work").
  • Ties that fact to your wedge in one sentence.
  • Asks one small question; no meeting ask in the first message.
  • Every draft stays in the SDR's inbox as a draft. Human sends.
Auto-sent AI outbound is burning the brand of every startup using it. Don't. Draft + human-in-the-loop is the only acceptable pattern.
04

Meeting prep agent

30 minutes before every call, Slack DM from the agent:

  • Who's on the call + their LinkedIn highlights.
  • Deal context: stage, amount, last exchange, commitments open.
  • Three goals for this call + three objections likely to surface.
  • One fresh data point that wasn't in the last call.
05

Call-coaching agent

Post-call, the agent transcribes, extracts MEDDIC/SPICED signals, and scores the rep against the playbook.

  • Did we identify the economic buyer?
  • Did we quantify the pain (dollars, hours, %)?
  • Did we get a specific next step with a date?
  • Weekly: the agent produces a rep-level coaching digest — strengths + one concrete thing to work on.
06

Deal-desk & proposal agent

Generates customized proposals, redlines back, and tracks every pricing exception.

  • Pulls the template + account data; produces a draft in Google Docs.
  • When the prospect redlines, the agent summarizes the changes and flags non-standard terms for legal (see Legal).
  • Every pricing discount is logged with reason; used later to detect drift ("we gave away 30% on every Q4 deal").
07

Pipeline hygiene agent

The most boring, highest-ROI agent. Keeps your CRM honest.

  • Stale-deal sweep: any opportunity with no activity in 14 days gets a pinged-rep reminder + a suggested next step.
  • Auto-logs: email threads, call summaries, doc views. No one types into the CRM.
  • Forecast call: Wed afternoon the agent produces a draft pipeline report. Rep adjusts. No more Friday panic.
08

Customer-lifecycle continuity

Sales → onboarding → support should feel like one company. Shared agents across stages.

  • Win-to-onboarding handoff doc is agent-generated from call transcripts + contract.
  • CSM sees the full history without having to read 40 emails.
  • QBR prep pulls from the data warehouse (usage, feature adoption, support volume) — no manual slides.
