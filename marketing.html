// MARKETING

Punch above your weight on a zero-dollar budget

You have no marketing team, maybe $2k/month, and need to generate pipeline. The goal isn't to drown the internet in AI slop — it's to build a small system of agents that amplify your voice, find real demand signals, and ship campaigns faster than any 10-person marketing team.

content engine SEO brand voice social SEM

Build the brand-voice brain first

Every agent below is useless without a tight brand layer. Nail this and your AI content stops sounding like everyone else's AI content.

01

Author a Brand Voice spec (the single most leveraged doc)

A one-pager that defines how your company sounds. Load it into every marketing prompt.

  • Voice attributes — 3 adjectives + 1 anti-adjective (e.g. "plainspoken, technical, slightly irreverent; never corporate").
  • Signature moves — e.g. "we use numbers, not adjectives"; "we name the competitor."
  • Banned phrases — "in today's fast-paced world", "delve", "leverage", "solution".
  • Golden examples — 5 paragraphs of copy you wish you'd written. This matters more than rules.
Feed this in as a system prompt preamble to every content agent. Store it in /brand/voice.md and version it.
Example: see examples/marketing.md — a filled-in voice.md for a fictional startup.
02

SEO Opportunity Scout

A research agent that finds queries you can actually rank for — not the head terms you never will.

  • Tool: Ahrefs or DataForSEO MCP + WebFetch for SERP inspection.
  • Weekly job: pull queries with KD<20, volume>50, and no strong answer in top-10.
  • Scores opportunity = (volume × intent) / (competitor authority + SERP saturation).
  • Drops the top 10 into a "write queue" with a recommended angle for each.
03

Content Engine (with a human spine)

Three sub-agents working against the write queue. Human writes the spine, AI fills the scaffolding.

  • Research sub-agent — gathers primary sources, stats, quotes. Outputs a structured brief.
  • Draft sub-agent — writes the article using brand voice + the research brief + a founder-authored thesis paragraph.
  • Editor sub-agent — checks banned phrases, reading level (~grade 9), internal link density, and one "earned insight" per 500 words.
Don't skip the thesis paragraph. The piece of the article that has to come from a human is the 3-5 sentence point of view. Everything else can be generated.
04

Distribution Fan-out Agent

One article → 6 distribution assets, each re-written for the platform (not just truncated).

  • LinkedIn post (founder voice, 1 hook + 3 beats + CTA).
  • X thread (6-9 tweets, punchier).
  • Email newsletter section.
  • YouTube script skeleton + thumbnail copy.
  • 2 slide-style graphics for Instagram/LinkedIn carousel.
  • A 30-second founder video teleprompter script.
# Platform rewrite prompt (LinkedIn)
Transform this article into a LinkedIn post.
Constraints:
  - Start with a 1-line hook that works even without the "see more".
  - No hashtag soup. Max 2 hashtags.
  - End with a question, not a CTA.
  - Preserve the founder's voice from <voice> above.
05

Demand-signal Listener

A scheduled agent that watches Reddit, HN, X, and niche Slack/Discord communities for people asking questions your product solves.

  • Scrapes with WebFetch (or Apify MCP) + classifies intent (question, complaint, recommendation-seeking).
  • Drops high-intent posts into Slack with a drafted, non-salesy reply for a human to review before posting.
  • Builds a weekly "voice of customer" digest the whole team reads.
06

Ad Creative & Landing Page Generator

For paid: use AI to produce 30 ad variants a week and let performance pick winners. For landing pages: generate variants off a single source-of-truth page-config.

  • Creative agent — generates 3 hooks × 3 value props × 3 CTAs = 27 ads against a single brief, then deduplicates semantically.
  • LP agent — consumes your page-config.yaml, emits React/Next components that match the design system (see Design).
  • Performance loop — once winners emerge, feed them back as "what's working" examples into the creative agent's next round.
07

Measurement: what to actually track

You'll generate 10x the output. That doesn't matter unless the right numbers move.

  • Organic: qualified signups from content (not pageviews).
  • Social: reply rate & profile visits (not likes).
  • Paid: CAC payback within 12 months (not CPC).
  • AI-specific: ratio of human edits to AI draft length — if editors rewrite >50%, your voice spec is weak.

// weekly cadence

  • Mon — SEO scout delivers write queue
  • Tue — 2 long-form drafts land for human edit
  • Wed — publish + fan-out
  • Thu — ad variant refresh + LP experiment
  • Fri — demand-signal digest + retro

// red flags

  • Articles ranking but no signups — wrong queries, fix the scout's scoring
  • Editors rewriting >50% — your voice spec is thin
  • Declining reply rate on social — over-automation, add more founder-written posts
